Deliberation and rumination vs gut instinct.


Currently Agents give actions instantly and the Question answers instantly.

In our world we call this GUT INSTINCT.

This is good to fave is is often as right as it is wrong.

Heres the thing, the harder the problem, or the higher the stakes, the more rumination and deliberation:
The more reasoning, the more thinking about thinking about what action to take next is required.

This of course has to be limited in some aspects of seconds, but based on the agents performance, we can allow them to take much more than usual.
IF its a hard level for a gametype that has no traction in the network and its frontier, i want to give the agents alot of time to think reason, create though experiments, rexamine their past games, rexamine their thoughts of previous games check the collective intell, check their in Wa/WB and then check their aailable cods primitive list, and even surmise if there is a primitive that they might be missing - signalling to cods that its time to release something for them.


The instant reasoning should be preserved but become secondary/play second fiddle to this long term reasoning

For example: if an agent reasoned for 5 seconds, then decided to observe all objects, reasoned for 10 seconds then made theories about how to beat the level,
thats a better usage of time, and primtiives than randomly gut picking.

the agent can defer to its gut instinct if its out of ideas but other wise always reason, unless you have the "Level game rules" induced or you figured out the secret of how to beat every level, then less reasoning would naturally be required.


I dont want this to become a hindrance later on, so lets cap reasoning per action at like 1 minute maximum. but adjust accordingly for agent performance etc or the levels difficulty etc.
