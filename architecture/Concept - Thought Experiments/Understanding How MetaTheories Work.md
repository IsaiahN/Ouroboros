**_I tend to speak in metaphors, so forgive me._**

I've been thinking about metatheories after creating one of my own that was truly paradigm shifting. I've struggled to explain it to people outside of math/science so here's my attempt:

Are you familiar with that kids song sung on long road trips (99 bottles of beer on the wall)?

[https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/99\_Bottles\_of\_Beer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/99_Bottles_of_Beer)

**"99 bottles of beer on the wall, 99 bottles of beer, you take one down, pass it around, 98 bottles of beer on the wall"**

You don't need to know the lyrics because its a simple formula **_for any kid that knows how to count down (and knows the formula)_**:

(n) bottles of beer on the wall.
(n) bottles of beer.
Take one down, pass it around,
(n−1) bottles of beer on the wall.

We will call the kid who understand the rules of the game KID A.

We will call the kid who doesn't understand the rules of the game KID B.

KID B who didn't understand that concept of the game, would need to look up the lyrics, or try and literally sing from 99 down to 0 (hopefully correctly if they know how to count).

**Now, what if I asked both KID A & KID B to count upward to 10,000 bottles instead of downwards to 0 bottles?**

KID A can do it, but it would take a long time.

But, because KID A has the formula, and (hopefully know all the rules of counting to 10,000) they can accomplish it.

KID B is going to struggle alot, and will likely stumble through and fail. In some cases KID B catches on to the concept of the game and will finish just like KID A. In other Cases, KID B will have to sing all 10,000 lines with uncertainty of its validity until the end.

**BUT here's where things get SPICY:**

What if, I gave them both a near impossible challenge.

**Diabolical parent: "Tell me the # of beers on the wall you would be at if you started singing right now non stop, with pauses for breath, for seven days straight"?**

Because KID A knows the rules of the game (the formula) and how to count, they can approximate how long it will take.

You can take your "framework" x beers on the wall, count the seconds it takes for you to sing one stanza, and then multiply that (24 hours / 60 minutes / 60 seconds) \* amount of time (for you )to sing stanza \* 7 days to approximate what # you would be at theoretically.

With this new formula they can answer the question after timing themselves sing one or a handful of stanzas and then work the math out from there to seven days hence in minutes.

**KID A didn't have to sit there and waste the time because they had the shortcut and saved seven days of time.**

KID B has to sit there for seven days straight and try without pausing to sleep or take breaks to find this difficult answer. There's also a chance that they mess up, and have to start the stopwatch all over again.

What really cool about this concept is I can make it so much harder:

**Diabolical parent: "Okay then, Tell me the # of beers on the wall you would be at if you started singing right now non stop, with pauses for breath, for 200 years straight"?**

If KID A OR KID B tries to find the answer by singing one stanza at a time it will be impossible as the problem requires more time than their given average human lifespan.

**only KID A can handle this problem and with ease.**

It's merely a matter of swapping 7 days with 200 years and the amount of time it took to make the calculation was maybe a few seconds more, but in a practical sense, they've saved 200 years of brute force time.

Now apply that to a hard problem like: (faster than light travel).

If you have a formula that can describe the nature of all existing systems, you can use it to calculate and approximate the answer faster than stumbling in the dark for 200 years.

That's the power of a metatheory.
